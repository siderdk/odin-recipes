Veggie Masala

A delicious healthy creamy dish

Veggie Masala is a vegetarian alternative to the famous Chicken Masala,
has all of the taste, with none of the meat

    Ingredients:
      The sauce:
    • 3 medium onions
    • 2tbsp garlic paste
    • 5 medium tomatoes
    • 1tbsp tomatoe paste
    • 1tbsp sugar
    • 1tbsp Ginger paste
    • Apple cider vinegar
    • 50gr of Butter
    • 1L water
    • Optional: 1 bell pepper
      • Spices:
      • Bayleaves
      • Oregano
      • Masala mix
      • Dried mint
      • Smoked paprika
      • Kurry
      • Chili
      The rice:
    • 1 Cup of rice
    • 2 Cups of water
    • 1 Anis flower
      The cream:
    • Cashew nuts -to taste
    • Heavy cream

    Steps to follow:

    1. The rice:
    1. Properly rinse the rice multiple times
    2. After rinsing your rice multiple times, put in the pot and with the water and let it sink, and add your anis flower
    3. when your sauce will be simmering, turn the heat on the rice pot, all the way up, until it starts boiling, then turn it to low heat
    4. when you notice that there is no liquid anymore, set it aside and let it rest
    2. The sauce:
    1. Pre-heat a nice pan, that you drizzeled with enough olive oil
    2. Roughly cutting the onions and putting them on the pan. The shape won't matter because we will blend everyting later
    3. Add in the garlic and the ginger pastes
    4. In the same fashion add the tomatoes and tomatoe paste
    5. Let everything sear nicely until golden brown
    6. Now you can add the sugar, remember to add salt to taste
    7. At this stage, you can throw in all the spices except the mint, which we will keep for the presentation
    8. Let everything fry on a medium-high heat, then add the vinegar, after which you should add the water and let it simmer nicely for about 40mn
    9. While everything is simmering you should remember start your rice, and to move to preparing the cream, you can come back here afterwards
    10. At this stage, your rice is ready, and so is the cream. After everything is nice and mushy, turn off the heat, and remove the bayleaves
    11. Transfer everything to a big bowl that can stand very warm liquides
    12. Use your hand mixer or blender to turn everything into a nice and smooth liquid
    13. With the help of a sieve, pour the filtered and smoother liquid back in the pan, and add the butter
    14. You can also add the bayleaves back, and make sure your thick sauce is nicely mixed with the butter
    15. now you can add your cream and crush the dried mint in your palm over the pan
    3. The cream:
    1. In a blender add: your heavy cream and cashews. Some people like to leave their cashew in water so they are softer, not me!
    2. Process everything until very smooth
    3. Drizzle in your sauce pot at the end
    4. Presentation:
    • In a deep dish pour your self some rice
    • Add your sauce to your liking
    • Add more crushed dried mint, and some of the cream to your liking
    • Enjoy!
